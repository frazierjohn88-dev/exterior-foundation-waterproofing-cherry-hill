Common Signs of Exterior Foundation Problems

Before diving into specific crack types, watch for these warning signals:

  • Uneven or sloping floors
  • Doors and windows that stick
  • Gaps between walls and ceilings
  • Bulging or bowing basement walls
  • Water seepage after rain

Types of Foundation Cracks and Their Significance

Vertical Cracks

These are the most common and typically least concerning. However, they still require professional foundation crack repair to prevent water infiltration.

Horizontal Cracks

These indicate serious structural issues, often caused by soil pressure or water damage. Immediate professional attention is crucial.

Diagonal Cracks

Usually resulting from uneven settlement, these cracks require evaluation by our Cherry Hill foundation experts.

Prevention and Maintenance Tips

Protect your foundation with these essential measures:

  • Proper grading and landscaping to direct water away from your foundation
  • Regular gutter maintenance and downspout extensions
  • Installation of French drains for improved drainage
  • Seasonal foundation inspections

When to Call a Professional

While minor cracks might seem manageable, professional assessment is recommended for:

  • Cracks wider than 1/8 inch
  • Multiple cracks in the same area
  • Cracks accompanied by water seepage
  • Any horizontal cracking
